## What new concepts did I understand?

### Python Basics

One thing I noticed today is that Python is quite easy to read. The syntax isn't something I'm worried about right now. What I need to focus on is understanding how different data types behave and when type conversion is needed.

I also made a few mistakes while trying simple programs. Earlier I would've just looked for the correct code, but today I tried reading the error messages first. They actually tell you what's wrong most of the time.

### Core Data Structures

The biggest thing I understood today is that different data structures exist for a reason.

Before today, I thought they were just different ways of storing data. Now I understand that each one is designed to make certain operations easier.

Arrays felt the easiest because the idea is straightforward. Linked lists took me a little longer to understand since the elements aren't stored together like arrays.

Stacks and queues were probably my favourite topics today because the LIFO and FIFO concepts were easy to remember once I related them to real-life examples.

Trees and graphs are still new to me. Right now I only understand their basic structure, so I'm not trying to memorize everything. I think they'll make more sense once I start solving problems on them.



## What computer/software engineering fundamentals did I learn today?

Something that stood out to me today is that writing code is only one part of programming.

How the data is stored also matters because it affects how easy or difficult different operations become.

I also understood why people keep saying that DSA is important. It's not about memorizing data structures it's about knowing which one makes sense for a particular problem.


## What changed in my thinking?

Before today, I looked at arrays, linked lists, stacks, queues, trees, and graphs as six separate topics that I had to study.

Now I see them as different tools. Each one has its own purpose, and learning when to use them is probably more important than just remembering their definitions.

I'm still at the beginning, but today's session gave me a much clearer picture of what I'll be learning over the next few weeks.
