# Day 12 - Building My First Frontend Project


## What did I learn?

Today was one of those days where I didn't sit and learn a lot of new theory, but I still learned a lot.

I spent almost the entire day working on my **Local Utility Downtime Tracker** project. It was nice because, for the first time, I wasn't just learning HTML, CSS, or JavaScript separately. I was actually using all three together to build something.

As I worked on different parts of the website, I could finally see how everything connects. HTML gave me the structure, CSS made it look the way I wanted, and JavaScript added the little interactions that made the page feel more like a real website.

I also spent quite a bit of time making small improvements. Sometimes it was fixing spacing, sometimes it was changing colors, and other times it was just cleaning up my code. These may seem like small changes, but together they made a big difference.

By the end of the day, the project looked much better than when I started, and that felt really satisfying.


# What challenges did I face?

Today's biggest lesson was that building a project takes a lot more patience than I expected.

There were times when I spent 20 or 30 minutes trying to fix something, only to realize that the problem was caused by a tiny mistake. It was a little frustrating, but every time I found the solution, I felt more confident.

I also kept making small design changes because I wanted the website to look cleaner. I realized that developers rarely get the design right on the first try. It's all about making small improvements until everything feels right.

# What new concepts did I understand?

## Building teaches you differently

One thing I noticed today is that learning from tutorials and building a project are two completely different experiences.

When you're watching a tutorial, everything looks easy because someone else has already solved the problems.

But when you're building on your own, you have to think about everything yourself,how the layout should look, where each section should go, why something isn't working, and how to fix it.

That process taught me much more than simply following along with a video.

### Debugging

I also started getting more comfortable with debugging.

Instead of immediately thinking something was too difficult, I tried checking my code carefully, changing one thing at a time, and testing it again.

It took time, but solving those problems on my own felt really rewarding.

### Paying Attention to Details

Another thing I learned is that good websites are built through lots of small improvements.

A little extra spacing, a smoother animation, better alignment, or a cleaner layout may not seem important on their own, but together they completely change how the website feels.


# What computer/software engineering fundamentals did I learn today?

Today helped me understand that software development is much more than writing code.

A project grows little by little.

You write some code, test it, find a bug, fix it, improve the design, reorganize the files, and repeat the process again.

I also continued using Git while making changes, which helped me understand why version control is such an important part of real world development.


# What changed in my thinking?

Before today, I thought the hardest part would be learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

Now I feel like learning the concepts is only the beginning.

The real challenge and honestly, the most enjoyable part is taking those concepts and using them to build something from scratch.

Even though I still have a lot to learn, today gave me confidence. A few days ago I was only reading about frontend development, and today I'm slowly building my own project.

It's not perfect yet, but every small improvement reminds me that I'm getting better with each day I practice.
