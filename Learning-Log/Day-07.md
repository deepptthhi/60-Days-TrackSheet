### Starting SmartExpiry

I spent some time thinking about what kind of project I wanted to build.

There are a lot of common beginner projects, but I wanted to work on something that felt useful to me.

That's how I ended up with **SmartExpiry**.

The idea is pretty simple. At home, we've forgotten about medicines that expired, found groceries in the fridge after they had gone bad, and even misplaced warranty cards or important documents. Remembering all these dates isn't easy because they're usually scattered in different places.

So I thought it would be useful to build something that keeps everything in one place.

The idea is to let users add items, save their expiry dates, and get reminders before they expire.

While planning the project, I noticed that today's OOP lesson actually fits really well. Items can be represented as classes, different item types can have their own behavior, and the inventory, dashboard, and notifications can all be kept separate instead of putting everything into one file.

I also spent some time updating the README. It helped me organize my thoughts and made the project feel more real instead of just being another idea in my notebook.
